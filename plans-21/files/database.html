Blacksky Labs · AI Database

Prism isn't a database.
It thinks.

Most databases store and retrieve. Prism understands. Built by Blacksky as an AI-native data layer, Prism moves data through tiers of increasing intelligence — from raw capture to structured knowledge to vector-indexed insight — with AI mediating every transition.

When your entity connects to Prism, it doesn't just query a database. It queries a living intelligence layer that gets smarter as more data flows through it.

Prism is its own product with its own mini-site. What follows is what you need to know for your AO entity.

Prism Tier Architecture
Tier 1
Capture
Raw intake & normalization
The first tier receives raw data from any source — API feeds, firehoses, manual ingestion, webhooks. Data is normalized into a consistent structure and time-stamped. Nothing is lost. Everything is captured. T1 is the archive layer — the source of truth for every record that has ever entered Prism.
MongoDB Raw ingestion Normalization Full archive Timestamp indexed
API Feed→ Firehose→ Webhook→ T1 Capture
↓
Tier 2
Structure
AI-mediated enrichment
AI mediates the transition from T1 to T2. Raw records are enriched, classified, and structured into relational form. Entities are extracted, relationships mapped, and metadata attached. T2 is where raw data becomes knowledge — queryable, structured, and semantically aware.
PostgreSQL AI enrichment Entity extraction Relationship mapping Classification
T1 Records → AI Enrichment → T2 Structure
↓
Tier 3
Intelligence
Vector-indexed insight
The intelligence tier. Structured T2 data is embedded into vector space — enabling semantic search, similarity reasoning, and meaning-aware retrieval across the full corpus. T3 is what your entity queries. When Judy answers a question about a congressional bill, she's pulling from T3 — where the data isn't just stored, it's understood.
Qdrant Vector embeddings Semantic search Similarity reasoning Real-time query
T2 Knowledge → Vector Embed → T3 Intelligence → Entity Query
Zero · The Intake Layer
Zero
NAS Edge Layer · Beneath Prism
Always On
Two Modes
Firehose Mode
Zero ingests a live data firehose — up to 20,000+ records per day — filtering and routing relevant signals down into Prism T1. Raw volume in. Relevant signal through. Everything else buffered and held.
Backup Mode
Zero acts as a cold backup layer for an existing Prism instance. Every record that enters Prism is mirrored to Zero. Full recovery capability. Zero data loss.
